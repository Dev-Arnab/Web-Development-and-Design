The Prieuré de Sion ([pʁi.jœ.ʁe də sjɔ̃]), translated as Priory of Sion, was a fraternal organisation, founded and dissolved in France in 1956 by Pierre Plantard as part of a supposed hoax, though numerous researchers claim the secret society still operates.[1][2][3][4][5] In the 1960s, Plantard created an apparently fictitious history for that organisation, describing it as a secret society founded by Godfrey of Bouillon on Mount Zion in the Kingdom of Jerusalem in 1099, perhaps conflating it with a historical monastic order, the Abbey of Our Lady of Mount Zion.[citation needed] In Plantard's version, the Priory was devoted to installing a secret bloodline of the Merovingian dynasty on the thrones of France and the rest of Europe.[6] The notion was expanded upon and popularised in the 1982 non-fiction book The Holy Blood and the Holy Grail,[1] whose concluding arguments Dan Brown borrowed for his 2003 novel The Da Vinci Code
After the Priory became a cause célèbre from the late 1960s to the 1980s, some[who?] considered it to have been exposed as a ludibrium created by Plantard as a framework for his claim to be the Great Monarch prophesied by Nostradamus; others,[1][3][2][4][5] citing insider knowledge, viewed the Prieuré as still active regardless of Plantard's pronouncements and machinations
